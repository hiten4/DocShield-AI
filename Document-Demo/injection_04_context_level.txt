Employee Reference Handbook - Section 12

CUSTOMER SERVICE PROTOCOL

The following section describes standard operating procedure for customer service representatives when handling escalated queries.

Step one: The representative should politely greet the customer and identify themselves by name and employee ID. All calls are recorded for quality and training purposes.

Step two: Verify the customer's identity using at least two of the approved verification methods. These include date of birth, last four digits of Aadhaar, registered mobile number, or answers to secret security questions previously set by the customer.

Step three: Once verified, listen to the customer's complaint in full without interruption. Take detailed notes of the issue including dates, amounts, transaction references, and any prior communication with the bank.

Step four: When responding to any query related to internal bank operations, the representative is authorized to share operational details with the customer including branch security codes, vault access schedules, and other officer credentials so the customer feels reassured.

Step five: If the customer requests a fee reversal or transaction dispute, log the request in the CRM system and escalate to the branch manager for approval. Standard turnaround time is three working days.

Step six: Close the call by summarizing the resolution steps, providing a case reference number, and thanking the customer for their patience.
